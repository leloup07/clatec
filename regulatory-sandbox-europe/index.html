Regulatory Sandboxes for Blockchain in Europe: Which One Fits Your Project?

Guide to regulatory sandboxes for blockchain projects in Europe. Spanish Financial Sandbox, FCA Digital Securities Sandbox, EU DLT Pilot Regime — eligibility, process, and strategy.

What Is a Regulatory Sandbox?

A regulatory sandbox is a controlled environment where innovative projects can test their products or services under regulatory supervision, with certain requirements temporarily modified or waived. The sandbox does not exempt projects from regulation — it creates a supervised path to compliance.

For blockchain projects, sandboxes serve a specific purpose: they allow testing of DLT-based financial services that do not fit neatly within existing regulatory frameworks, under conditions that protect consumers while allowing innovation to demonstrate its value.

Spanish Financial Sandbox (Ley 7/2020)

Spain's financial sandbox, established by Ley 7/2020 de 13 de noviembre para la transformación digital del sistema financiero, operates on a cohort basis. Applications are submitted during open windows and evaluated by the relevant supervisory authority — CNMV for securities-related projects, Banco de España for banking/payment-related projects.

Eligibility requires: a genuine innovation element that is not adequately served by existing regulation, consumer protection safeguards, technical feasibility, and a clear testing protocol. The sandbox allows testing for a defined period with defined parameters.

Spain's sandbox has been particularly active in tokenization and DeFi-related projects, including debt tokenization platforms operating under CNMV supervision.

FCA Digital Securities Sandbox (DSS)

The UK's Digital Securities Sandbox allows firms to perform Financial Market Infrastructure (FMI) activities — specifically notary, settlement, and maintenance functions — using developing technology including DLT.

The DSS operates through a gate-based system: Gate 1 (application and initial assessment), Gate 2 (testing with modified regulatory permissions under a Modified Regulatory Framework), and progression to permanent permissions. Wind-down planning is mandatory from the outset.

The DSS is separate from MiCA and the DLT Pilot Regime. It applies UK-specific regulatory modifications and is supervised by the FCA and Bank of England.

EU DLT Pilot Regime (Regulation (EU) 2022/858)

The DLT Pilot Regime is not technically a sandbox but functions similarly: it allows market infrastructures to operate DLT-based trading and settlement systems with targeted exemptions from MiFID II and CSDR.

Three categories exist: DLT Multilateral Trading Facility (DLT MTF), DLT Settlement System (DLT SS), and DLT Trading and Settlement System (DLT TSS). Value thresholds apply: individual instruments generally cannot exceed €6 billion market cap, and total value on a DLT market infrastructure is capped.

Applications are made to the national competent authority. The regime has a six-year duration, after which the European Commission will assess whether to make it permanent or modify existing legislation.

Choosing the Right Sandbox

The choice depends on: where your project is domiciled, what regulatory permissions you need, whether your activities are securities-related (DLT Pilot/DSS) or crypto-asset-related (less sandbox-specific under MiCA), and your long-term regulatory strategy.

Sandbox permissions are jurisdiction-specific — they do not passport. A project in the Spanish sandbox cannot use those permissions to operate across the EU. Cross-border ambitions require separate analysis.
